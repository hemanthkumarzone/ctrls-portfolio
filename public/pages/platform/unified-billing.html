Frequently asked questions
What exactly is unified billing in AI FinOps? +
Unified billing consolidates all AI infrastructure invoices — from multiple cloud providers, bare-metal vendors, and on-premise systems — into a single normalized view. It eliminates manual reconciliation, enables accurate cost allocation, and provides a real-time financial picture of your entire AI compute footprint.
How does the platform handle different invoice formats across vendors? +
The platform uses AI-powered invoice parsers that ingest structured (CSV, JSON) and unstructured (PDF) invoices from all major providers. A normalization engine maps vendor-specific line items to a common taxonomy — compute hours, storage, networking, and licensing — regardless of source format.
Can we do chargeback to internal teams or external clients? +
Yes. The platform supports both showback (cost visibility with no financial consequence) and chargeback (actual billing allocation). Configurable rules allow split-cost across multiple owners, percentage-based allocation, and direct client invoicing for managed AI service providers.
How does anomaly detection work in billing? +
The platform uses statistical baselines and ML models trained on your historical billing patterns. Deviations beyond configurable thresholds trigger real-time alerts to cost owners via Slack, email, or PagerDuty. Anomalies are auto-classified as spikes, new resources, or pricing changes.
Does it support reserved instance and savings plan tracking? +
Yes. The platform tracks coverage, utilization, and expiration dates of all committed use discounts (CUDs), reserved instances (RIs), and savings plans across AWS, GCP, and Azure. It alerts on underutilized commitments and recommends rightsizing or selling unused capacity on cloud marketplaces.
How is on-premise GPU cost handled in unified billing? +
On-premise cost is ingested via DCIM integrations or manual upload. The platform models total cost of ownership including power (kWh × PUE), hardware depreciation, networking, and cooling — presenting on-prem cost in the same $/GPU-hour unit as cloud, enabling true apples-to-apples comparison.
What compliance and audit capabilities does the platform provide? +
The platform maintains a full audit trail of all billing data ingestions, cost allocation changes, and policy modifications with user-level attribution. Exportable reports support SOC 2, ISO 27001, and internal finance audit requirements. Data retention policies are configurable per regulatory jurisdiction.
How long does it take to integrate all billing sources? +
Most cloud providers connect via native API integrations within hours. On-premise and custom vendor sources using CSV or PDF ingestion typically take 1–3 days to configure. Full multi-source unification for an enterprise org with 8 billing sources is typically live within 1–2 weeks.
