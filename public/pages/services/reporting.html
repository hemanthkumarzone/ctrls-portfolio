Custom report builder

Pre-built templates get you 80%
of the way. The builder gets
you the other 20%.

When your reporting need is specific to your organisation's structure, the custom builder lets you compose exactly the report you need — without writing a line of SQL.

1

Choose your data sources

Select which cost dimensions to include — categories, teams, workload types, time periods, and any custom dimensions you've defined. Mix and match freely.

2

Add metrics and calculations

Pick from 80+ pre-built metrics — total spend, efficiency scores, waste ratios, budget variance, forecast accuracy, savings realised — or define custom formulas using our metric builder.

3

Set layout and visualisations

Choose from pre-designed layouts (executive brief, detailed analysis, operational digest) and configure which visualisations — trend charts, comparison tables, category breakdowns — appear where.

4

Configure recipients and schedule

Define who receives the report, by what channel (email, Slack, webhook, BI tool), on what cadence — daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly — and at what local time.

5

Preview, approve, and activate

See a live preview populated with your real data before activating the schedule. Adjust any element and preview again. Once approved, the report runs on autopilot.

Custom reports are versioned — every change creates a new version, and recipients can always view the previous version for comparison. No report is ever accidentally overwritten.

Platform integration

Reports that close the loop
on every platform pillar

Reporting is the accountability layer — it turns every platform capability's output into a documented record of what happened, what was saved, and what remains to be done.

PillarWhat reporting delivers for this capabilityStakeholder outcome
📡 Monitoring Monitoring data is the primary input to all reports — every report draws on the cost, usage, and anomaly telemetry collected by the monitoring layer. Reports make monitoring data accessible to stakeholders who don't live in the platform — translating signals into business language. Executives and finance teams receive monitoring insights in their preferred format without needing platform access. Engineers receive their team's monitoring data distilled into actionable weekly digests.
⚙️ Optimization The Optimisation Impact Report documents every action taken — right-sizing applied, model routing changes, reserved capacity purchased — with before/after cost comparison and verified savings. This report provides the ROI evidence that justifies continued investment in the FinOps programme. Finance teams can reconcile optimisation savings in their books. Leadership can see FinOps ROI clearly. Engineering teams see which of their pending recommendations have the highest savings potential.
🔀 Orchestration Orchestration actions — workload routing decisions, spot instance migrations, scheduling policy changes — are reported in the engineering and compliance reports with full audit trails. Cost savings from orchestration are attributed and tracked separately from right-sizing savings. Platform teams have documented evidence of every orchestration decision for compliance audits. FinOps teams can attribute savings to specific orchestration actions and prove programme value.
🗑️ Waste elimination The Waste Elimination Tracker report is the central accountability document for the waste elimination programme — showing newly found waste, actioning status, verified savings, and backlog trend. It transforms waste elimination from an invisible background activity into a measurable, communicable business outcome. FinOps teams have a running scorecard they can present to leadership. Finance can incorporate verified waste savings into cost reduction reporting. Engineering teams see which of their workloads contributed to waste found and waste eliminated.

Failed report generations are retried automatically up to 3 times with exponential backoff. If all retries fail, an alert is sent to your platform administrator and the report owner. Failed deliveries (e.g. a Slack webhook returning an error) are retried separately from generation failures — a report that generated successfully but failed to deliver is retried without regenerating. All failures and retries are logged in the report audit trail. You can also manually trigger a report regeneration at any time from the reporting dashboard.

There is also a self-service report portal where stakeholders can log in with just an email OTP to access all reports they've been granted access to, without a full platform account.
